Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
General Digital Safety Tips

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Always turn on 2FA for your college email, social media, and banking apps to add an extra layer of security beyond just your password.

Verify Links Before Clicking: Hover over links in emails or SMS messages to check the actual destination URL. Phishing scams often use URLs that look almost exactly like the real ones.

Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Never log into your bank account or college portal while connected to unsecured public Wi-Fi networks (like at cafes or railway stations).

Update Your Software Regularly: Keep your laptop's operating system, phone apps, and antivirus software up to date to patch known security vulnerabilities.

Limit Social Media Oversharing: Do not post photos of your college ID card, admission letters, or travel tickets, as scammers can use this data for identity theft.

Download Apps from Official Sources Only: Only install applications from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store, and always review what permissions the app is requesting before hitting install.

Financial & UPI Safety Tips

Never Enter a PIN to Receive Money: Remember the golden rule of UPI: You only ever need to enter your UPI PIN when you are sending money from your account, never to receive it.

Don't Google Customer Care Numbers: Scammers actively manipulate Google search results to show fake helpline numbers. Always find customer care numbers directly inside the official app or on the official website of the service provider.

🚨 Emergency Reporting Channels
If you or someone you know falls victim to a cybercrime or financial fraud, report it immediately:


National Cyber Crime Portal: Visit cybercrime.gov.in 


Cyber Crime Helpline: Dial 1930 (Available 24x7)
