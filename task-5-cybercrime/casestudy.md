Case Study: The "Failed Fee" UPI Scam

What the crime is: This is a classic UPI "Collect Request" fraud. It is a type of online payment fraud where scammers manipulate victims into entering their UPI PIN to send money, disguised as a process to receive a refund for a failed transaction.

Who is targeted: College students are frequent targets. Freshmen, in particular, often handle large transactions for tuition or hostel fees for the first time and are prone to panic if a payment gets stuck.

How it happens (Step-by-Step):

The Trigger: A first-year student, Rohan, attempts to pay his ₹15,000 semester mess fee online. The college portal glitches, and the money is deducted from his bank but doesn't show up on his student dashboard.

The Lure: Panicking, Rohan searches online for "Payment Gateway Customer Care" and dials the first number he sees. Unfortunately, scammers have manipulated search results to show their fake helpline number.

The Trap: The fake "executive" calmly assures Rohan that his money is safe and promises an instant refund. He instructs Rohan to open his UPI app (like GPay or PhonePe).

The Execution: The scammer sends Rohan a UPI "Collect Request" for ₹15,000. He tells Rohan to click "Pay" and enter his UPI PIN, falsely claiming that entering the PIN is required to "verify the bank account to process the refund."

The Theft: Believing he is receiving money, Rohan enters his secret PIN. Instantly, another ₹15,000 is drained from his account. The scammer immediately disconnects the call and blocks Rohan's number.

Consequences: Rohan loses a total of ₹30,000, causing severe financial strain for his family. Beyond the financial loss, he experiences high levels of stress, anxiety, and embarrassment, affecting his focus during his first month of college.
